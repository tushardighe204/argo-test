My Music Stats

How to enable Last.fm scrobbling & visualise your Last.fm data with My Music Stats

This article describes how to enable scrobbling on Android devices. I don't have an iOS device to test, but I believe the same steps apply there as well. All the screenshots have been taken on my Android phone. You can open them in new tab.

Create a Last.fm account (Skip if you already have a Last.fm account)

Since My Music Stats depends on Last.fm data, the first thing that you have to do is to create a Last.fm account (if not already done). It is a free service, however, you can choose to enable Subscription to get advanced features like last week listening report & an ad-free experience.

Go to Last.fm & fill the details to create your account.

Download Simple Last.fm Scrobbler

This article is based on a free, open source & 100% ad-free Android app known as Simple Last.fm Scrobbler. I use it personally because it simply works & requires minimal configuration. It sits on your device & does its job quitely in the background. Once configured, you don't even need to open the app ever.

Once it is installed, we need to configure it.

Follow below steps to configure Simple Last.fm Scrobbler

Simple Last.fm Scrobbler Configuration

Open Simple Last.fm Scrobbler.

Tap User credentials.

Enter your Last.fm account details.

Configure Playback threshold.

This setting means the duration of the song that must be heard to register as a played track. 50% means half of the song must be finished. You can change the setting here.

For this, go back to the app's homepage & tap the Options.

Now tap the Scrobble point & adjust the slider accordingly. For most users, 50% is sufficient.

Configure Your Music Player

Simple Last.fm Scrobbler supports all the major music player apps. You can check the list on the Play Store page or their GitHub repository.

It is not possible to describe the steps for all the music players. So I have decided to explain the configuration options for the music player apps that I use the most. You can follow the same steps for almost any supported music player.

Configure Black Player

Go to Black Player's Settings & then tap Metadata.

Check the Scrobble Music box. If it is already selected, keep it checked.

Configure Rocket Player

Go to Rocket Player's Settings & then tap Sound.

Now tap the Simple Last.fm radio button. If it is already selected, keep it checked.

Your configuration is now complete & you can start listening to your songs from your favorite Android music players & analyse your music data with My Music Stats :-)
